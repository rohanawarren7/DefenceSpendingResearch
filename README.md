# Defence Spending Research

--Intro--
Our world is changing rapidly, however we're seeing a shift in global power in this moment which will likely be reflected on as a stark inflection point in geopolitcal history. 
The USA has continued to be a hegemonic power across *** economic, political, militarial and cultural power. With an ability to throw their weight around in the world that is 
currently unmatched financially. 

Through the apparent chaos that's both depicted on the news and experienced on a high street level and at the fuel pump, my aim is to see if we can see order in the chaos. 
These changes in the behaviour of growing and existing dominant powers have knock on effects to other areas of the global economy.

I want to explore the political and militarial landscape to find trends that could be used for further research, which could be useful for deriving insights in areas such as finding 
opportunities for investment purposes, developing knowledge within academia or simply to further current strategic understanding.

--Areas of Interest--
*Can the sentiment trajectory of U.S. National Security Strategy/NDS documents predict the direction of force posture changes 3–5 years ahead?*
I will be applying NLP sentiment analysis and topic modeling. The literature confirms this methodolgy is viable: transformer-based models (FinBERT derivatives, RoBERTa) are already used for geopolitical media sentiment, and military applications of LLMs explicitly include sentiment analysis for information operations.
