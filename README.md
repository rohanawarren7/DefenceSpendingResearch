# Defence Spending Research

## Intro
Our world is changing rapidly, however we're seeing a shift in global power in this moment which in future will be reflected on as a stark inflection point in geopolitcal history. 


Through the apparent disruption that's depicted on the news, experienced on the high street and felt at the fuel pump, my aim is to see if we can see order in the chaos. 
These nation state level changes in existing and growingly dominant powers have knock on effects to other areas of the global economy.

I want to explore the political, economic and militarial landscape to find trends that could be used to understand the influence of the US 2026 NDS. 
This could be useful for deriving insights in areas such as finding investment opportunities, developing knowledge within academia or simply to further current strategic understanding of world affairs.

## Why Does This Matter?
Regardless of the stakeholder, the research questions are relevant as the outcomes of what we're exploring have direct impacts on the production and transit of key resources in this globalised world.
It appears in Europe we have grown too passive in our assumptions that allied dominant powers would remain a part of the regional infrastructure in perpetuity, as was provised in the years after WWII ended when the Western World was assisted and protected by our North American partners which steam rolled our current world order of a US led Western alliance.

The USA has long continued to be a hegemonic power across economic, political, militarial and cultural domains. However...what do you do when your ally isn't your friend?
